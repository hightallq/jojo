JOJO'S BIZZARE ADVENTURES

What is it?

Jojo's Bizarre Adventure is a Japanese manga series created by Hirohiko Araki. The story follows the adventures of the Joestar family, whose various members discover they are destined to take down supernatural foes using unique powers that they possess.

Why has the Joestar's saga become so popular?

The series is known for its wild and creative characters, over-the-top action sequences, and outlandish fashion sense. It is divided into eight distinct story arcs, each following a different member of the Joestar family and their allies as they face off against a variety of powerful and eccentric enemies.

HIROHIKO ARAKI

Who is this?

Hirohiko Araki is a Japanese manga artist and the creator of the popular manga series "JoJo's Bizarre Adventure". He was born on June 7, 1960 in Sendai, Miyagi Prefecture, Japan.

Araki began his manga career in 1980 with the one-shot "Buso Poker" and went on to create several other short manga series before starting "JoJo's Bizarre Adventure" in 1987. The series follows the adventures of the Joestar family, spanning multiple generations, as they battle supernatural villains using their unique "Stand" abilities.

Araki's distinct art style, which combines western comic book influences with Japanese manga aesthetics, has made him a highly influential figure in the manga industry. In addition to "JoJo's Bizarre Adventure", he has also created other successful manga series such as "Baoh", "Cool Shock B.T.", and "Rohan at the Louvre".

What's Araki's style?

Araki's art is heavily influenced by Western art and culture, particularly the work of artists like Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Gustav Klimt. He often incorporates elements of classical art and architecture into his manga, giving his work a sense of grandeur and timelessness.

Another hallmark of Araki's style is his attention to detail. He carefully renders every aspect of his characters and settings, from their clothing and facial expressions to the smallest background details. This attention to detail adds depth and richness to his work and makes it feel more immersive.

Finally, Araki is known for his use of color, which he often employs to create a sense of mood and atmosphere. He frequently uses bold, saturated colors to create striking visual contrasts, and his color choices are always carefully chosen to enhance the emotional impact of his scenes.

Overall, Araki's art style is a unique and eclectic blend of influences that sets his work apart and makes it instantly recognizable.
